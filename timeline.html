ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE MILESTONES

1948

First autonomous robots were created by William Grey Walter in the form of two ‘tortoises’ named Elmer and Elsie. These tortoises navigated without human help, using light and bump sensors.

1950

Alan Turing created the Turing Test, a method of measuring a machine’s intelligence

1956

The Dartmouth Conference was held and started the term “artificial intelligence”

1961

William C Dersch showcased IBM Shoebox, a computer that recognized 16 voice commands and did math

1966

Joseph Weizenbaum created the first chat bot, ELIZA

1968

Shakey, the first personal assistant robot, was presented at the Artificial Intelligence Center of Stanford Research Institute. Shakey responded to voice commands and performed sequences of actions to complete tasks

1971

Stuart Russel and Peter Norvig wrote the first program to successfully play chess

1995

The Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon created Navlab5, a vehicle that used sensors to steer itself, driving from Pittsbery to San Diego

1997

Deep Blue by IBM defeated the champion chess player Garry Kasparov and NASA landed Sojourner, an autonomous robot and vehicle that used lasers and cameras to navigate, the foundation of modern LIDAR technology

2000

Honda showcased ASIMO, a wireless robot that can navigate environments, respond to commands and behaviors (such as shaking your hand when offered), , recognize faces, provide verbal responses in different languages, and react to noises

2011

Siri, the first modern virtual assistant was added as a feature to the iPhone 4S and IBM Watson beat Ken Jennings and Brad Ruther at Jeopardy

2013

IBM made Watson’s API available to software providers to incorporate into apps and services

2022

OpenAI releases GPT-2
