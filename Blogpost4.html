How is AI going to affect employment?

Posted on: March 1, 2023

The topic of artificial intelligence (AI) and its impact on employment is a common topic of discussion and has raised concerns about the potential for job loss in various industries. However, it is important to recognize that the benefits of AI far outweigh the negatives. While it is true that some jobs may be displaced by automation, the adoption of AI is also expected to drive significant job growth and creation in various industries. According to the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), it is estimated that there will be a net positive effect on employment, with approximately 85 million jobs being displaced and 97 million new roles being created that are adapted to AI and machine learning.

Therefore, it is essential for business executives to consider the relationship between job growth and the adoption of AI in various industries, and to understand the potential implications of AI on the workforce. By staying informed and understanding the impact of AI on employment, executives can make better-informed decisions about how to prepare their organizations and employees for the changing job market, and ensure their companies are able to thrive in the long run.

AI's Impact on Employment

Job Replacement

The integration of AI technology in various industries has led to significant changes in the way businesses operate. While AI has been hailed for its ability to automate many manual, repetitive, and predictable tasks, it has also raised concerns about job loss. We will examine the impact of AI on employment using the manufacturing and retail industries as examples.

The Manugacturing Industry:

The integration of AI in the manufacturing industry has demonstrated its ability to automate numerous manual, repetitive, and predictable tasks, thereby reducing the need for manual labor. This has resulted in increased production efficiency and improved product quality, but has also shown an effect on job loss. Many manual tasks, such as product inspection and monitoring of production processes, are also being performed by AI systems, which will lead to cost savings for these companies and give them a competitive edge in the market. As a result, the automation potential is around 60% and the estimated share of manufacturing workers at risk of losing their job is 14%.

The Retail Industry:

In the retail industry, AI technology is rapidly being adopted to enhance customer experiences, optimize supply chain operations, and streamline inventory management. The aim of integrating AI in this sector is to automate these repetitive, and predictable tasks, such as price comparison, product recommendations, and inventory management. Additionally, the use of AI-powered chatbots and virtual assistants, to offer quick and personalized support to customers has also reduced the need for human customer service representatives. Overall, there is a 55% automation potential in the retail industry and is expected to cause 15% of unemployment in that industry’s workforce.

The importance of these examples is that job replacement will happen, but it isn’t going to happen all at once. It will be an incremental process and when employees become concerned about their job security and future with a company, it can lead to higher stress and a decrease in morale and engagement in their work. For businesses, the cost of stress is significant, as approximately 297 million working days are lost annually due to stress-related absenteeism. Moreover, job insecurity can also manifest as a lack of motivation, reduced productivity, and a negative attitude toward their job and the company. Additionally, employees may start actively seeking new job opportunities, leading to an increase in turnover and the need for frequent recruitment and training of new employees before AI can be fully implemented.

The replacement of employees with artificial intelligence (AI) technology can also have a detrimental impact on a company's reputation and brand image. Such a transition can elicit negative perceptions among the public and other stakeholders, causing harm to the company's relationship with them. The heavy reliance on AI technology in place of human workers may be perceived as a lack of empathy and concern for employees as the contribution to unemployment may be seen as unethical. Additionally, the replacement of employees with AI can cause tension and conflict in the workplace, and damage employee morale and engagement. As a result, making it harder to attract top talent.

Companies must have thorough discussions about how they want to proceed with implementing AI. It's important that they remain transparent about the potential impact of AI on jobs and to engage in open and ongoing dialogue with employees and other stakeholders about the future of work. By doing so, they can help to build trust and ensure that the transition to an AI-driven workforce is managed in a responsible and equitable manner.

Job Creation & Enhancement

The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) into various industries is transforming the job market and will create numerous new job opportunities. Despite popular beliefs, AI is not just causing job loss, in fact, it’s actually leading to the creation of jobs in several fields.

AI has created a need for new job roles that specifically focus on facilitating the development, implementation, and maintenance of AI systems and solutions. This includes positions such as data scientists, machine learning engineers, and AI architects, as well as professionals with skills in cloud computing, big data, and cybersecurity. These jobs are highly technical and require a deep understanding of machine learning algorithms, data analysis, and computer science. As a result, the averaged out demand for these types of professionals is expected to grow by 36% within the next few years.

With AI’s hand in creating new jobs, significant economic benefits will arise. Firstly, the creation of new jobs will help reduce unemployment, leading to increased consumer spending and economic growth, which in turn will drive even more job creation. Additionally, the new jobs created by AI tend to be higher-skilled and higher-paying positions, which can help raise the standard of living and further improve the overall economy.

Further, companies should care about AI-driven job growth for several reasons. One of the primary benefits is increased task completion speed. AI-powered automation will be able to perform tasks faster and more accurately than humans, such as data entry and record keeping, which will allow employees to focus more on crucial, higher-value responsibilities, thus creating a more valuable and productive workforce. As a result, improved efficiency, better performance, reduced costs, a higher-skilled workforce, and increased innovation will help the company achieve its goals and objectives more effectively. This can lead to improved business operations and enhanced competitiveness, helping companies stay ahead and maintain a strong position in their respective markets.

In addition, the requirement for employees to learn about AI will offer greater opportunities for job enhancement. It is important to look at this from a eudaimonic well-being approach as well. For those who care about inner growth and reaching their highest potential, gaining difficult, in-demand, and cutting-edge skills will provide them with the ability to reach feelings of self-actualization. This can help create a more committed, positive work environment and foster a culture of continuous learning and growth.

Moreover, as employees gain such skills and develop expertise, the door to career advancement opportunities and higher salaries widens. For example, an employee now skilled in using AI to automate tasks may be given additional responsibilities, such as overseeing the implementation of AI solutions throughout the company. Another employee who has expertise in data science may be given a leadership role in the company's data analytics team. These examples of AI-driven job growth will allow more employees to experience a sense of fulfillment, satisfaction, and security in their work. Thus, with expanded skill sets, and advances in companies’ hierarchical ladder, companies can expect a much higher-performing and motivated workforce.

Overall, it is crucial for businesses to have a strategic approach toward understanding the impact of AI on jobs. This requires staying up-to-date on current and future trends in job loss and growth in various industries. By having a forward-looking perspective, executives can make more informed decisions involving their workforce to support the long-term success of their companies. This includes developing strategies to reskill and upskill employees to stay competitive, and investing in technologies and solutions that support the workforce in the face of changing job demands.
