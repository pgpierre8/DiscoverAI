Why an AI-skilled workforce will be your best investment!

Posted on: March 1, 2023

As AI will continue to increasingly impact organizations, the question arises as to what the most critical investments organizations must make in order to maintain a competitive and agile workforce, and overall position in the marketplace. The answer is clear: upskilling and reskilling. Reskilling refers to teaching current employees new skills to prepare them for new or alternative roles, while upskilling involves developing the skills that employees already have. By proactively utilizing these approaches, businesses can help their workforce bridge the current and future skill gaps to adapt to the evolution of AI. An adaptable workforce, seeking upskilling and reskilling opportunities, enables organizations to remain at the forefront of technological change, increasing their likelihood of success for the coming years.

According to the World Economic Forum (2020), by 2025, more than 1.1 billion employees will be radically affected by AI and require upskilling or reskilling. For those who are set to remain in their roles, the share of core skills will change by 40% and more than 50% of all employees will need reskilling.

Based on these statistics, a critical point that must be acknowledged is long-term commitment. It is imperative that both individuals and organizations remain committed to these initiatives, and make career development a key component of the future workforce given that AI has no plans on slowing down any time soon. In order to achieve this goal, companies must take a multi-faceted approach - promoting a continuous learning culture, implementing targeted training programs and educational initiatives, and directly partnering with AI research and startups to stay informed about industry trends and best practices.

Targeted Training Programs and Educational Initiatives

The most popular method of upskilling and reskilling (that we already see) will be the investment in targeted training programs around AI; providing employees with the knowledge and skills they need to effectively use and work with AI technologies. These programs can take many forms, including AI fundamentals courses, AI application courses, AI programming and development courses, and AI leadership and strategy courses. Each of these programs has a different focus, ranging from providing a foundational understanding of AI technologies to teaching employees how to build and implement AI systems and navigate the ethical and governance implications of AI.

However, it is important to note that implementing these training programs comes at a cost, which can vary depending on several factors such as the type of training program, its duration, its focus, the number of employees to be trained, and the delivery method. Therefore, companies must begin budgeting such costs to identify financial feasibility and base program selection on such measures. Some programs, such as online courses and MOOCs, can be low-cost or free, while others, such as customized in-house training or certification programs, can be much more expensive. It is also important to consider the opportunity cost of employees being away from work during training and any potential losses in productivity.

AI Research and Startup Partnerships

Directly partnering with AI research and startups is another unique opportunity for companies to upskill and reskill their employees. By partnering with research institutions and startups, companies can access cutting-edge knowledge and expertise in AI that would otherwise be difficult or costly to obtain on their own. This can provide organizations with a deeper understanding of how AI is being used across various industries.

Partnering with AI startups can also provide employees with hands-on experience working with real-world AI applications, which can be an invaluable opportunity for them to learn new skills and apply their knowledge in a practical setting. Additionally, partnering with startups can help employees to network and collaborate with other professionals in the AI field, which can expand their knowledge, enhance their skills, and increase their exposure to new opportunities that align with their personal interests and organizational objectives.

From an overall organizational perspective, by leveraging these opportunities, companies can expect improved productivity and job satisfaction. Not only that, investing in these initiatives can demonstrate a commitment to employee development and growth, thus, improving employee morale and retaining top talent.

On the other hand, these partnerships can present several challenges for companies. Firstly, finding the right partner is crucial. With how diverse AI is, there are numerous startups and research organizations that offer AI training and development programs. However, companies need to be careful in their selection process, considering factors such as the partner's alignment with their needs, goals, and values. Additionally, companies need to have the necessary resources and funding available to support such partnerships. Partnering with these organizations often requires significant investment in terms of both time and money, and companies need to ensure that they have the necessary resources and funding to support these initiatives. Finally, companies need to be mindful of the impact of their partnerships on both internal and external stakeholders, including employees, customers, and partners. Maintaining internal and external alignment is essential to ensure that the partnerships are successful and deliver the desired outcomes.

Overall, these discussed reskilling and upskilling initiatives will help organizations stay ahead of the curve, create a culture of continuous learning, and ensure their employees have the skills they need to succeed. However, it is important for companies to carefully consider the challenges and opportunities involved in such investments in order to maximize the benefits and ensure continuous success.
