What's The Significance?

AL/ML is entering the workforce and HR/OD Practitioners need to be prepared to give them a warm welcome. Businesses are seeing the long-term ROI AL/ML will have and no longer view them as entirely experimental. 35% of companies based on a BMI study report using AI in their business, while 42% are exploring the use of AI. AI/ML will leave a lasting impact on the workforce across a variety of industries, which in turn will impact HR/OD Practitioners who are responsible for sourcing, developing, & enriching employees experience while being agents of the business. Being vigilant on how AI/ML is currently integrated into the following: workflows across the HR/OD industry, your current place of employment, and across various industries will be vital. Stay up-to-date on the technical aspects & any new AI phenomenon's that are anticipated to provide positive outcomes for businesses. Primarily to ensure you are not surprised when they enter your company & you are sought out for insight or need to help integrate the new AI/ML. So with that let's dive deeper into what this really means for us as practitioners through our relevant blog posts and overview below.

Need to Forecast New Roles

Work displacement and/or job replacement by AI is an impact practitioners will need to be prepared to handle. Primarily on how it will create a need for new job roles in a company. With these new job roles we will structure the job design, criteria, job duties, & determine base pay ranges all before a candidate is even sourced.

Identify How AI Enriches Employees' Experience

It's important for practitioners to understand the value AI brings to a business, but more so the value AI can & will have on employees experience. Having this identified for any AI/ML integration will be useful when combatting any resistance from impacted employees & decrease the potential of low morale
